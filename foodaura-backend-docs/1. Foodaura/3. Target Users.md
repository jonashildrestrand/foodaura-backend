# Target Users

## Primary Persona
**Name:** The Household Planner

**Who they are:** A person (25–40) who manages most of the cooking and shopping for a household of two or more people — a partner, kids, or flatmates — each with different nutritional needs, goals, or schedules. They want to eat intentionally but aren't a nutritionist, and the mental overhead of making it all work together is the main obstacle.

**Pain points:**
- Planning meals that work for everyone without cooking separate dishes
- Not knowing which foods actually support each person's goals
- Shopping lists that require manual consolidation across multiple recipes
- Recipes designed for one person that don't scale to their household — or to the nights people eat separately

**Jobs to be done:**
- Generate a weekly meal plan that accounts for the whole household's goals and eating schedule
- Get a consolidated shopping list automatically, without piecing it together by hand
- Discover recipes that fit their household by default, not after extensive filtering

---

## Secondary Persona
**Name:** The Goal-Conscious Single

**Who they are:** A single person (22–35) with a clear health or fitness goal — building muscle, eating better, managing weight — who wants their eating to support that goal but finds calorie tracking apps obsessive and generic recipe apps unhelpful. They want structure without rigidity.

**Pain points:**
- Generic meal planning tools don't account for their specific goals or body
- Calorie and macro tracking feels like a second job and isn't sustainable
- Hard to know whether what they're eating is actually moving the needle
- Manual planning from scratch every week takes time they don't want to spend

**Jobs to be done:**
- Get a personalized meal plan that reflects their goal without requiring constant logging
- Build a sustainable weekly routine around food, not a temporary diet
- Shop efficiently for one, with no waste and no guesswork

---

## Out of Scope Users
- **Hardcore trackers** — users who want granular control over every calorie or macro; Foodaura presents nutrition as context, not a scoreboard
- **Dieters on a program** — users following a prescribed plan (keto, Whole30, etc.) who want a tool that enforces it; Foodaura works with goals, not diet rules
- **Community seekers** — users looking to share recipes, follow others, or engage in a social feed; Foodaura has no community features
- **Meal kit or delivery users** — users who want food sourced or delivered for them; Foodaura is a planning tool, not a commerce platform
