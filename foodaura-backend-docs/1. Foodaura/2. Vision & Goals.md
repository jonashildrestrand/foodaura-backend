# Vision & Goals

## Vision
A world where every household can eat in alignment with their individual goals — without needing a nutritionist, a cookbook, or hours of weekly planning.

## Problem Statement
People want to eat better, but the gap between intention and action is enormous. Knowing you want to "eat healthier" or "lose weight" doesn't tell you what to cook on Tuesday, how much protein you actually need, or what to put in your shopping cart. That complexity multiplies in a household where each person has different nutritional needs, goals, and schedules — not everyone eats together every day. Existing tools either obsess over calorie counting, overwhelm with generic recipes, or plan for a single person and ignore household dynamics entirely. Foodaura closes that gap by turning each person's goals into a shared, flexible plan that adapts to how the household actually lives.

## Goals
- Users can generate a personalized weekly meal plan in under 2 minutes
- Shopping lists are automatically derived from the meal plan with no manual input
- Recipe discovery is filtered by user profile and goals by default
- Manual meal planning is fully supported for users who prefer control
- Nutritional guidance is presented as context, not as a tracking burden
- Recipe ingredients automatically scale to match the user's nutritional needs and goals
- Households with multiple members can plan together, with each person's profile accounted for
- Recipes scale for the full household when eating together, and for individuals on separate days

## Non-Goals
- No social feed, sharing, or community features
- No food delivery or meal kit integration
- No strict diet programs or prescriptive eating plans
- No manual calorie or macro logging
- No restaurant or takeaway recommendations

## Success Metrics
- Weekly active users who complete at least one meal plan per week
- Time from sign-up to first generated meal plan (target: under 5 minutes)
- Week-over-week retention of active planners
- Percentage of meal plans that generate a shopping list
- User-reported confidence in knowing what to eat (qualitative, via surveys)
