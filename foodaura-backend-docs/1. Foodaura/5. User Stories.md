## User Profile & Goals

- As a **new user**, I want to provide my personal details and primary goal during setup so that every meal plan and recipe recommendation is calibrated to me from day one.
  - Acceptance: Setup flow collects biological sex, age, weight, height, activity level, and primary goal (e.g. build muscle, lose weight, eat better); nutritional targets are derived from these inputs and stored against the profile; raw targets are not surfaced unless the user explicitly seeks them.

- As a **Goal-Conscious Single**, I want to update my profile details as my goal changes so that the app continues to plan around where I actually am, not where I started.
  - Acceptance: Profile fields are editable after initial setup; changes propagate to future meal plan generation; existing meal plans are not retroactively altered.

---

## Household Profiles

- As a **Household Planner**, I want to invite someone to my household so that their nutritional profile and goals are included in our shared meal plan.
  - Acceptance: An invitation can be sent via email or username; inviting an existing user sends them a join request; inviting a new user prompts them to create a Foodaura account before joining; the invitee is not a household member until they accept.

- As a **new user**, I want to accept a household invitation and set up my own profile so that my goals and preferences are reflected in the household's plan from day one.
  - Acceptance: Accepting an invitation triggers the standard profile setup flow; the user's profile is linked to the household upon completion.

- As a **Household Planner**, I want to remove a member from the household so that the household reflects who actually lives there as things change.
  - Acceptance: Any member can be removed by the household owner; removed members are excluded from future planning; their personal account and preferences are unaffected; existing meal plans are not retroactively altered.

- As a **Household Member**, I want to leave a household I belong to so that I am no longer included in that household's planning.
  - Acceptance: Any non-owner member can leave a household; leaving excludes them from future planning for that household; their personal account, profile, and preferences are unaffected; existing meal plans are not retroactively altered.

---

## AI Meal Plan Generation

- As a **Household Planner**, I want to generate a full weekly meal plan in one tap so that I go from goals to a complete plan without spending time figuring out what everyone should eat each day.
  - Acceptance: Plan is generated in under 2 minutes; all household members' nutritional profiles and goals are factored in; the generated plan covers all meal slots for the selected week.

- As a **Household Planner**, I want to regenerate a single day's meals without affecting the rest of the week so that I can quickly fix a day I'm not happy with.
  - Acceptance: Individual days can be regenerated in isolation; the rest of the plan remains unchanged; regenerated meals still account for all relevant household profiles.

- As a **Goal-Conscious Single**, I want to generate a weekly meal plan for myself so that I have a structured, goal-aligned routine without having to think about it each day.
  - Acceptance: Plan generation works for single-member households; plan reflects individual profile and goal; result is available in under 2 minutes.

---

## Manual Meal Planning

- As a **Household Planner**, I want to manually assign recipes to any meal slot in the week so that I can incorporate meals I already have in mind or override what the AI suggested.
  - Acceptance: Any meal slot can be filled or replaced by selecting a recipe manually; existing AI-generated slots can be overridden without triggering full regeneration; nutritional context is shown per slot during editing.

- As a **Household Planner**, I want to build an entire week's plan by hand without using AI generation so that I have full control over what the household eats.
  - Acceptance: Users can start with an empty week and populate it slot by slot; the app does not force AI generation before manual editing is available.

---

## Recipe Discovery

- As a **Goal-Conscious Single**, I want to browse recipes that already match my profile and goals by default so that I don't have to manually filter through irrelevant results to find something that fits.
  - Acceptance: The default recipe view is filtered by the user's profile and goals; additional filters are available (cuisine, cook time, ingredients); the catalogue is curated — no community-submitted or user-generated recipes.

- As a **Household Planner**, I want to search recipes by name or ingredient so that I can quickly find something specific when I have a recipe or ingredient in mind.
  - Acceptance: Free-text search returns relevant results from the curated catalogue; results still respect the user's profile filters unless manually overridden.

---

## Recipe Scaling

- As a **Household Planner**, I want recipe ingredient quantities to automatically adjust to who is eating that meal so that I don't have to calculate portions manually when cooking for different numbers of people.
  - Acceptance: Ingredient quantities scale based on the household members assigned to that meal; scaling respects each person's nutritional targets, not just an even per-serving multiplier; the scaled quantities are visible on the recipe view.

- As a **Goal-Conscious Single**, I want recipes to scale to my individual nutritional targets so that I'm eating the right amount for my goal, not a generic serving size.
  - Acceptance: Scaling for single-member meals uses the user's personal nutritional targets; the scaled recipe makes clear how it maps to the individual's goal.

---

## Automatic Shopping List

- As a **Household Planner**, I want a consolidated shopping list automatically generated from my weekly meal plan so that I don't have to manually cross-reference every recipe and piece the list together by hand.
  - Acceptance: The shopping list is generated directly from the active meal plan with no manual input; quantities for the same ingredient across multiple recipes are combined into a single line item; the list can be organised by category (produce, protein, pantry, etc.).

- As a **Household Planner**, I want the shopping list to update when I change the meal plan so that it always reflects what I'm actually cooking that week.
  - Acceptance: Changes to the meal plan (added, swapped, or removed recipes) are reflected in the shopping list; previously checked-off items are preserved where possible.

---

## Household Scheduling

- As a **Household Planner**, I want to mark which household members are home and eating together on each day of the week so that the meal plan and recipe scaling reflect how we actually live, not an assumption that everyone always eats together.
  - Acceptance: Each day can have a different subset of members marked as eating together; the meal plan adapts to produce shared meals on joint nights and individual portions on separate nights; scheduling affects recipe scaling automatically.

- As a **Household Planner**, I want to set a repeating weekly schedule template so that I don't have to re-enter who's eating together every single week.
  - Acceptance: A default weekly schedule can be saved and applied to new weeks; the schedule can be adjusted for a specific week without overwriting the template.

---

## Nutritional Context

- As a **Goal-Conscious Single**, I want to see a simple nutritional summary per meal and per day so that I can feel confident my plan is supporting my goal without turning food into a numbers game.
  - Acceptance: Each meal and day displays protein, carbs, fat, and calories as supporting context; the display is calm and non-prominent — the plan itself is the primary interface; users cannot log meals or track against targets.

- As a **Household Planner**, I want to see nutritional context per meal slot when building or editing a plan manually so that I can make informed choices without needing to know nutrition by heart.
  - Acceptance: Nutritional context is visible during manual meal planning; it appears per slot as supporting information, not as a pass/fail scoring system.

---

## Recipe Preferences

- As a **Household Planner**, I want to mark recipes as liked or disliked so that the app stops suggesting meals we don't enjoy and gives more weight to the ones we do.
  - Acceptance: Any recipe can be liked or disliked from the recipe view or meal plan; disliked recipes are excluded from AI generation and deprioritised in discovery; liked recipes are favoured in generation and discovery; preferences are stored per user and carry across all households they belong to.

- As a **Household Planner**, I want to see all household members' preferences when planning so that I can avoid meals that someone dislikes, even when they're not the one making the plan.
  - Acceptance: When planning for a household, each member's liked and disliked recipes are surfaced to the planner; preferences are not exposed outside the households the user belongs to.

- As a **user**, I want to mark specific ingredients I don't enjoy so that recipes containing those ingredients are deprioritised for me regardless of which household I'm planning for.
  - Acceptance: Ingredient-level dislikes can be set from the profile or recipe view; they influence recipe discovery and AI generation for the user across all their households.

---

## Meal Plan History

- As a **Household Planner**, I want to view my past weekly meal plans so that I can look back and reuse a week that worked well for the household.
  - Acceptance: Past meal plans are accessible and browsable by week; any previous plan can be loaded as the starting point for a new week; history is available once the user has completed at least one prior week.

- As a **Goal-Conscious Single**, I want to reuse a past meal plan week so that I don't have to start from scratch when I had a week that felt right.
  - Acceptance: Loading a past plan as a new week copies the meal slots; the user can edit or regenerate individual slots before committing; the past plan itself is preserved unchanged in history.

---

## Notifications

- As a **user**, I want to see a notification when I receive a household invitation so that I don't miss it and can respond promptly.
  - Acceptance: A notification is created when an invitation is sent to the user; it appears in the in-app notification centre as unread; it links to the invitation so the user can accept or decline directly.

- As a **Household Owner**, I want to be notified when someone accepts my invitation so that I know the household is complete without having to check manually.
  - Acceptance: A notification is created for the owner when an invitee accepts; it appears as unread in the owner's notification centre.

- As a **Household Owner**, I want to be notified when a member leaves the household so that I can update the meal plan accordingly.
  - Acceptance: A notification is created for the owner when a member leaves; it appears as unread in the owner's notification centre.

- As a **Household Member**, I want to be notified if I am removed from a household so that I am aware of the change.
  - Acceptance: A notification is created for the removed member when they are removed; it appears as unread in their notification centre.

- As a **Household Planner**, I want to be notified when AI meal plan generation is complete so that I know when the plan is ready to review.
  - Acceptance: A notification is created for the requesting user when the AI service finishes generating a meal plan; it appears as unread and links directly to the generated plan.

- As a **user**, I want to mark notifications as read so that my notification centre stays useful and doesn't surface things I've already seen.
  - Acceptance: Any notification can be marked as read individually; there is an option to mark all as read at once; read notifications remain visible but are visually distinct from unread ones.
