# Dictionary

Shared definitions for domain-specific language used across Foodaura. When in doubt, use these terms as written — consistent naming reduces ambiguity in design, engineering, and product conversations.

---

## Goal

A user's primary health or dietary intention, set during onboarding and stored on their profile. Examples: *build muscle*, *lose weight*, *eat better*. A goal drives the user's nutritional targets but does not prescribe a specific diet program or eating rules.

---

## Household

The core planning unit in Foodaura. A group of people — typically living together — who share a meal plan and shopping list. A household is created and managed by one **User** (the account holder) and can contain any number of **Household Members**. Most Foodaura features operate at the household level.

---

## Household Invitation

A request sent by a **Household Owner** to bring another person into the household. An invitation can be sent to an existing **User** (who accepts to join) or to someone new (who must create a Foodaura account before joining). Until accepted, the invitee is not a member of the household.

---

## Household Member

A **User** who belongs to a **Household**. Every member has their own account, **Nutritional Profile**, and **Goal**. Members' profiles are used in meal plan generation, recipe scaling, and preference-aware planning. A household can have one **Household Owner** and any number of additional members.

---

## Household Owner

The **User** who created a **Household** and is responsible for managing it — inviting and removing members, and overseeing the meal plan. Every household has exactly one owner. The owner is also a **Household Member**.

---

## Household Schedule

The weekly configuration that defines which **Household Members** are eating together on each day. Drives both the structure of the **Meal Plan** (shared vs. individual meals) and the quantities produced by **Recipe Scaling**. Can be set as a repeating template and overridden per week.

---

## Meal Plan

A week's worth of recipes assigned to **Meal Slots**. The primary output Foodaura produces for a household. A meal plan can be generated by AI, built manually, or a combination of both. Each household has one active meal plan per week.

---

## Meal Slot

A specific position in a **Meal Plan**, defined by a day and meal type (e.g. *Monday dinner*, *Wednesday lunch*). A meal slot is either empty, filled by AI generation, or filled by manual recipe selection. Slots are independent — regenerating or editing one slot does not affect others.

---

## Nutritional Context

The per-meal and per-day display of protein, carbohydrates, fat, and calories within the app. Presented as ambient supporting information to help users feel confident in their plan. Not a tracking interface — users cannot log meals against it or be scored against a target. The plan itself is the product; nutritional context is the explanation behind it.

*Not to be confused with **Nutritional Targets*** — targets are the underlying data; context is how that data is surfaced to the user.

---

## Nutritional Profile

The personal details of a **User** or **Household Member** — age, weight, height, activity level, and **Goal** — used to calculate their **Nutritional Targets**. Nutritional profiles are the input; nutritional targets are the output.

---

## Nutritional Targets

The protein, carbohydrate, fat, and calorie values derived from a person's **Nutritional Profile**. Used internally to guide **Recipe Scaling** and meal plan generation. Not surfaced as a scoreboard or daily tracking target. Raw targets are only shown if the user explicitly seeks them.

---

## Recipe

A curated dish in the Foodaura catalogue. Includes a title, ingredient list with base quantities, preparation steps, and nutritional data per serving. Recipes are authored and maintained by Foodaura — not user-submitted or community-sourced. The catalogue is the only source of recipes used in planning.

---

## Recipe Preferences

Per-user signals about which recipes and ingredients a user wants more or less of. Includes recipe-level likes and dislikes, and ingredient-level dislikes. Preferences are stored per **User** (not per household) and apply across every household the user belongs to. Disliked recipes are excluded from AI generation and deprioritised in discovery; liked recipes are favoured. This is a personalisation signal, not a social or rating feature.

---

## Recipe Scaling

The automatic adjustment of a recipe's ingredient quantities based on who is eating that meal and each person's **Nutritional Targets**. Not a simple serving-count multiplier — quantities are derived from individual targets so each person gets the right amount for their goal. Scaling adapts per meal slot based on the **Household Schedule** for that day.

*Not to be confused with manually adjusting serving sizes* — scaling in Foodaura is always goal-aware and household-aware.

---

## Shopping List

A consolidated, de-duplicated list of ingredients automatically derived from the active **Meal Plan**. Quantities for the same ingredient across multiple recipes are summed into a single line item. Organised by category (e.g. produce, protein, pantry). Generated with no manual input; updates when the meal plan changes.

---

## User

A person with a Foodaura account. Every person in a household — including the **Household Owner** — is a user with their own login, **Nutritional Profile**, **Goal**, and **Recipe Preferences**. A user can belong to multiple households and can be invited into an existing household by its owner.
