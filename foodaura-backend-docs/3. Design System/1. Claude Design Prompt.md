# Claude Design Prompt

Use the prompt below in Claude Design to generate the Foodaura frontend design system and key screens.

---

## Prompt

Design a complete visual design system and key screens for **Foodaura** — a household meal planning web app.

---

### What Foodaura is

Foodaura is a food companion for households who want to eat with purpose. It helps users go from personal health goals to a full weekly meal plan, then straight to a consolidated shopping list — without calorie tracking, restrictive diets, or hours of manual planning. It handles households with multiple members who each have different goals, dietary needs, and schedules.

The app is **not** a calorie tracker, not a diet enforcer, and not a meal kit service. Nutrition is shown as calm, supporting context — not as a scoreboard.

---

### Users

**Primary — The Household Planner (25–40)**
Manages cooking and shopping for a household of 2+ people with different goals. Wants to eat intentionally but doesn't want the mental overhead. Needs the app to handle household complexity so they don't have to.

**Secondary — The Goal-Conscious Single (22–35)**
Has a fitness or health goal (build muscle, eat better, manage weight) but finds calorie trackers obsessive. Wants structure without rigidity and a sustainable weekly routine, not a temporary diet.

---

### Brand personality

- **Calm and grounded** — not gamified, not anxiety-inducing, no red alerts or achievement badges
- **Warm and purposeful** — food is nourishing and enjoyable, not a numbers problem to solve
- **Modern and clean** — uncluttered layouts, generous whitespace, clear hierarchy
- **Trustworthy** — the user delegates planning to Foodaura; the UI must feel reliable and considered
- **Not clinical** — avoid sterile health-app aesthetics (white/green medical palettes, harsh iconography)

---

### Design direction

**Color palette:**
- A warm, earthy primary — think terracotta, warm amber, or deep sage; something that evokes real food and kitchens, not a tech product
- A light, warm neutral background (off-white or warm grey, never pure white)
- One accent for interactive elements (buttons, active states, links) that is confident but not aggressive
- Muted tones for nutritional context — it should be visible but not dominant

**Typography:**
- A humanist sans-serif for body and UI (good options: Plus Jakarta Sans, DM Sans, Inter with tighter tracking)
- Optional: a slightly characterful serif or display face for headings and recipe titles to add warmth
- Clear hierarchy: meal plan slots, recipe names, and nutritional summaries each need a distinct visual weight

**Spacing and layout:**
- Generous padding — this is a planning interface, not a dashboard
- Card-based layout for recipes and meal slots
- A weekly grid for the meal planner that is readable at a glance on both desktop and mobile
- Mobile-first: the shopping list and meal plan views must work well on a phone (these are used in kitchens and supermarkets)

**Nutritional information:**
- Always secondary — displayed as a quiet row of small tags or a subtle summary bar, never as a large chart or progress ring
- Labels like "protein", "carbs", "fat" rather than raw numbers front-and-centre
- Calorie totals can appear but should not dominate

---

### Key screens to design

1. **Onboarding / Goal Setup** — A multi-step flow where a new user sets their goal (e.g. build muscle, lose weight, eat better), personal details (age, weight, height, activity level), and dietary preferences. Should feel lightweight and conversational, not like a medical intake form.

2. **Weekly Meal Plan** — The main screen. A 7-day grid with breakfast, lunch, and dinner slots. Each slot shows a recipe card (name, thumbnail, quick nutritional summary). Empty slots are inviting, not alarming. Includes a "Generate plan" CTA and per-slot controls (regenerate, swap, remove).

3. **Recipe Card / Detail** — A recipe page showing the name, a hero image, scaled ingredients (amounts already adjusted for the household), steps, and a nutritional summary. The scaling is invisible — it just shows the right amounts without explaining the math.

4. **Recipe Discovery** — A browsable catalogue pre-filtered to the user's profile. Shows recipe cards in a grid or list. Filters available (cuisine, cook time, dietary tags) but not overwhelming — the default view is already relevant.

5. **Shopping List** — A clean, categorised list (Produce, Protein, Dairy, Pantry, etc.) derived automatically from the meal plan. Items can be checked off. Designed for one-handed use on a phone while shopping.

6. **Household Setup** — A screen to view and manage household members, each with their own name, goal, and profile. Shows who is eating together on which days (household scheduling).

---

### Technical constraints

- The app is **server-side rendered** (Go templates, no client-side framework). The design system must be implementable in plain HTML and CSS — no React-specific patterns.
- Interactions (hover states, transitions, checked states) should be achievable with CSS alone or minimal vanilla JS.
- No dependency on a third-party component library; define a bespoke system.

---

### Deliverables requested from Claude Design

1. **Color tokens** — primary, secondary, accent, background, surface, text, muted, nutritional, border, error
2. **Typography scale** — font choices, sizes, weights, and line heights for headings (H1–H4), body, caption, and label
3. **Spacing scale** — base unit and step system (e.g. 4px base, steps: 4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64)
4. **Core components** — recipe card, meal plan slot, nutritional summary bar, shopping list item, household member chip, primary button, secondary button, input field, tag/badge
5. **Key screen mockups** — at minimum: Weekly Meal Plan (desktop + mobile), Shopping List (mobile), Recipe Detail (desktop)
6. **Iconography direction** — style recommendation (outline vs filled, stroke weight, corner radius) and a sample set covering: meal plan, recipe, shopping list, household, calendar, settings, generate/AI, like/dislike
